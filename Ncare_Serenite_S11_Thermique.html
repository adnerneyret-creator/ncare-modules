Why contrast works best for stress
Hot then cold creates a vascular pump — vasodilation followed by vasoconstriction. This mechanical action drains inflammatory markers and flushes cortisol metabolites from tissue.
Cold then hot ends in relaxation. Better for sleep. The warm finish activates the parasympathetic system — body temperature drops afterward, signalling sleep onset.
Consistency matters more than intensity. 3× per week of 2-minute cold + 5-minute warm has more long-term effect than one extreme session per week.
Your thermal toolkit.
Three protocols.
From simplest to most complete. Start wherever you are.
Level 1 — Cold finish (daily, 30 seconds)
Warm shower as normal. Last 30 seconds: cold. Step out without rewarming. You already have this from Module S07 of Maintain. Same protocol — new context: stress management, not just metabolism.
Level 2 — Contrast shower (3× week, 8 minutes)
2 min hot → 30 sec cold → 2 min hot → 30 sec cold → 2 min hot → 1 min cold. The alternation is the mechanism. Timer in the next slide guides you through it automatically.
Level 3 — Heat + cold (1–2× week, 30 minutes)
20 min sauna or hot bath (40°C) followed by 2–3 min cold shower. The sequence: cortisol drops during heat, norepinephrine spikes during cold, mood elevation lasts 4–6 hours. Best for high-stress days.
⚠️ Start at Level 1. Cold tolerance builds over 1–2 weeks. Don't force Level 3 immediately. The benefit is from consistency, not from suffering.
