Why this window is so fragile
Hospital support ends abruptly. Round-the-clock monitoring, medication delivery, professional care — all gone in one discharge. Your body notices.
Steroids taper off. If you were on oral steroids in the hospital, their anti-inflammatory effect fades over 5-10 days. Hidden inflammation can return.
Life returns. Stairs, shopping, stress, sleep disruption. The real world tests your lungs in ways the hospital didn't.
How a flare-up
really starts.
It rarely happens overnight. There's almost always a warning — if you know how to read it.
The 3 early signals — in order of appearance
Day -3 to -5: Sputum changes. Your mucus becomes thicker, more abundant, or changes color (yellow, green). This is your bronchi reacting to a new irritation or infection.
Day -2 to -3: More rescue inhaler use. You reach for your blue inhaler 3, 4, 5 times a day instead of your usual 0-2. Your body is asking for more bronchodilation.
Day -1 to 0: Breathlessness escalates. What was manageable yesterday feels harder today. Walking to the bathroom leaves you winded. Sleep is interrupted.
If you catch the early signals — especially sputum changes — you can often reverse the flare-up at home with a short course of antibiotics or steroids. Catch it late, and you end up in the ER.
What to track
every single day.
60 seconds a day. That's the price of staying home.
🫁 Breathlessness — on a scale of 0 to 10
How hard is breathing today compared to yesterday? Same, worse, or much worse? Small changes matter. A jump from 3 to 5 is more significant than staying at 6.
🌡️ Sputum — color and amount
Clear or white is normal. Yellow means possible infection. Green or brown means likely bacterial infection — call your care team. Also note if you're producing more than usual.
💨 Rescue inhaler — count every puff
How many times did you use your blue inhaler today? 0-2 puffs is stable. 3-5 puffs is a warning. More than 6 puffs in 24h means you need to call someone.
😴 Sleep quality — one simple score
Did you sleep through the night? Did breathlessness wake you up? Did you need to sit up to breathe? Poor sleep is often the first hidden sign of trouble.
⚡ Energy level — a 0-10 check
How did you feel today overall? Some days will be 7s, some days 4s. The pattern matters more than one bad day. A steady decline over 3 days is a real signal.
Your 7-day promise.
Not to us. To yourself.
60 seconds a day for 7 days. That's all it takes to know whether you're stable, drifting, or in trouble. And your care team sees the same signals you do — in real time.
What time of day will you do your check-in?
Pick a time anchored to something you already do every day. That's how habits form. If you miss a day, don't quit — just come back the next day. 5 out of 7 is already a win.
