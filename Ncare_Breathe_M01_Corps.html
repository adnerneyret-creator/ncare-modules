A COPD flare-up acts on 3 levels simultaneously
Narrowed airways. Inflammation reduced the caliber of your bronchi. Even with treatment, full recovery takes 4 to 6 weeks.
Exhausted respiratory muscles. Your diaphragm worked in overdrive. It needs progressive rebuilding — rushing makes it worse.
Weakened immune defense. Your airways are temporarily less protected against new infections. Prevention matters more than usual.
Your treatment handles today's inflammation. Breathe's job is to prepare you for tomorrow — by giving you the tools to prevent the next flare-up.

🫁 Breathing — your first line of defense
Pursed-lip breathing and diaphragmatic breathing reduce the sensation of breathlessness by 30 to 40%. It's not a relaxation trick. It's respiratory physics.
💊 Inhalers — technique changes everything
90% of patients use their inhaler incorrectly. Poor technique reduces drug delivery by up to 70%. Learning to use them right matters as much as the medication itself.
🚶 Movement — your shield against relapse
15 minutes of progressive daily walking cuts readmission risk in half. Even when breathing feels hard. Especially when breathing feels hard.
🧠 Anxiety — the loop you need to break
Breathlessness triggers anxiety. Anxiety amplifies breathlessness. It's a documented vicious cycle. Breaking it early changes your trajectory.
