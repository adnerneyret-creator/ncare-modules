Rebound is predictable.
Which means you can intercept it before it happens.
Most people notice they've regained weight when the scale tells them. But the biological signals that caused the rebound started 2 weeks earlier — in sleep quality, activity levels, and appetite patterns.
The rebound sequence
Week –2: Sleep quality drops. Energy decreases. Mood becomes slightly lower. These feel unrelated to weight — they're not.
Week –1: Sugar cravings increase. Activity spontaneously drops. Meals become less structured. The pattern is forming.
Week 0: The scale moves. But it's the end of the sequence — not the beginning. Acting here is late.
The opportunity: Act at Week –2. That's what the 3 signals in this module allow you to do.
The 3 signals.
Each one is a 2-week early warning. Each one has a specific response.
Signal 01
Sleep degrades
Poor sleep raises ghrelin and lowers leptin — the exact hormonal combination that drives hunger and fat storage. Sleep degradation precedes weight regain by 10–14 days.
Immediate response
Fix sleep before fixing food. No screens after 9pm for 5 days. Everything else will follow.
Signal 02
Sugar cravings increase
A spike in sweet cravings signals that blood glucose regulation is becoming less stable. This is usually caused by sleep deprivation or stress — not diet failure.
Immediate response
Add protein to every meal for 3 days. Protein stabilises blood sugar and reduces sweet cravings within 48–72 hours.
Signal 03
Activity spontaneously drops
When energy balance tilts negative — more calories in, fewer out — the brain reduces spontaneous movement first. You feel less inclined to walk, stand, move. This is metabolic adaptation in real time.
Immediate response
Reinstate the post-meal walk (Module 02) for 5 consecutive days. That's enough to reset the activity baseline.
