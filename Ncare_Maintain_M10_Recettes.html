Inflammation and weight.
The hidden connection.
One causes the other. And both drive rebound.
The inflammation-weight cycle
Inflammatory foods → trigger low-grade chronic inflammation → disrupt leptin signalling → brain stops receiving "I'm full" signal → you eat more.
Excess fat tissue → releases inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, TNF-α) → increases insulin resistance → body stores more fat even at the same calorie intake.
The loop closes itself. Inflammation drives weight gain. Weight gain drives inflammation. Breaking one breaks the other.
Anti-inflammatory eating doesn't require a special diet. It requires shifting the ratio — more protective foods, fewer inflammatory ones. Not perfection. Direction.
5 foods that protect.
Keep these in your kitchen. They do the work passively.
🫒
Olive oil
Oleocanthal blocks the same enzyme as ibuprofen. 2 tbsp/day measurably reduces CRP (inflammation marker).
🫚
Turmeric
Curcumin inhibits NF-κB — a key inflammation trigger. Add black pepper to increase absorption by 2000%.
🐟
Fatty fish
Omega-3 EPA/DHA directly convert to anti-inflammatory resolvins. 2× per week is the minimum effective dose.
🫘
Legumes
Feed Bifidobacteria — gut bacteria that produce butyrate, a short-chain fatty acid that reduces gut inflammation.
🫐
Berries (blueberries, raspberries)
Anthocyanins cross the blood-brain barrier and reduce neuroinflammation. Frozen berries retain 90% of the benefit. No excuses.
3 to reduce.
Not eliminate.
Direction, not perfection. These three drive 80% of dietary inflammation.
↑ Inflames
Ultra-processed foods (UPF) — emulsifiers, seed oils, additives disrupt gut barrier and trigger immune activation.
↓ Swap for
Whole foods with 5 ingredients or fewer. If you can't picture it growing or being made — reconsider.
↑ Inflames
Refined sugar and HFCS — spikes insulin, feeds inflammatory bacteria, directly activates NF-κB pathway.
↓ Swap for
Whole fruit (fructose + fibre = different metabolic effect). Dark chocolate 70%+. Dates in small amounts.
↑ Inflames
Industrial seed oils (sunflower, canola, soybean) — high omega-6 ratio shifts the omega-6/omega-3 balance toward inflammation.
↓ Swap for
Olive oil for cold use. Coconut oil or butter for high-heat cooking. Avocado oil when needed.
One recipe.
5 ingredients.
10 minutes.
The anti-inflammatory bowl — your weekly anchor meal.
🥗 The Maintain Bowl
⏱ 10 min · 1 serving · All 5 protectors in one bowl
100g cooked lentils or chickpeas
Large handful of leafy greens (spinach, rocket, kale)
½ avocado or a handful of walnuts
1 tbsp olive oil + squeeze of lemon
Pinch of turmeric + black pepper
Combine. Drizzle. Season. Done. Protein: add a boiled egg, tinned salmon, or grilled chicken if desired.
This bowl contains: olive oil (oleocanthal) + turmeric (curcumin) + lentils (butyrate) + greens (polyphenols) + avocado (monounsaturated fat). That's 5 anti-inflammatory mechanisms in 10 minutes.
