The 3 most common mistakes
Not exhaling first. If your lungs are full of air, there's no room for the medicine. You spray — it bounces back.
Breathing in too fast. Fast inhale = drug sticks to your mouth and throat, never reaches your lungs.
Not holding your breath. 10 seconds of hold = the medicine settles deep in your airways. Skip it, and most of it leaves on the exhale.
Why technique matters
more than dose.
You can double the dose and still get less medicine than someone using it right.
What happens when technique is right
Deep delivery. Medicine reaches the small airways where inflammation lives — not just your throat.
Sustained action. Proper breath-hold lets the drug settle. Protection lasts the full 12 or 24 hours as designed.
Fewer side effects. When the drug goes where it should, less stays in your mouth. Less oral thrush, less hoarseness.
A patient with bad technique and maximum dose gets less protection than a patient with correct technique and half the dose. Technique is the multiplier.
The 5 steps
that change everything.
Memorize them. Practice them. Teach them to someone else.
1️⃣ Shake and prepare
For MDI inhalers, shake 4-5 seconds. For dry powder inhalers, load the dose as instructed. Remove the cap. Stand or sit upright — never lying down.
2️⃣ Exhale fully — away from the inhaler
Breathe out completely. Empty your lungs. This is the step 80% of patients skip. If your lungs are full, the medicine has nowhere to go.
3️⃣ Seal your lips — then inhale slowly
Place the mouthpiece between your teeth, close your lips tightly. Start inhaling slowly, then press the canister (MDI) or continue inhaling forcefully (DPI). Aim for 3-5 seconds of slow, deep inhale.
4️⃣ Hold your breath — count to 10
Remove the inhaler. Hold your breath for 10 seconds — or as long as comfortable. This is when the medicine settles deep into your airways.
5️⃣ Exhale gently — and rinse if needed
Breathe out slowly through pursed lips. If you're using a steroid inhaler, rinse your mouth with water and spit — this prevents oral thrush.
