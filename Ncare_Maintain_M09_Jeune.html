Most people already fast 8–10 hours overnight. 16/8 simply extends that natural window by delaying breakfast 2–3 hours.
What happens during the 16-hour window
Hours 0–8: digestion completes, insulin drops to baseline. Body shifts from glucose-burning to fat-burning mode.
Hours 8–12: glycogen stores deplete. Fat oxidation increases. Ketone production begins mildly.
Hours 12–16: autophagy activates — cellular cleaning process. Growth hormone rises, protecting muscle mass during the fast.
Is 16/8 right for you?
Three profiles. One honest answer each.
✓ Good fit
You skip breakfast occasionally and feel fine. You eat dinner before 8pm. You don't have hypoglycemia or diabetes requiring frequent meals.
⚡ Proceed with care
You exercise in the morning — eat within 2 hours of your session. You have a history of disordered eating — IF can trigger restriction patterns. Start with 12/12 instead.
✗ Not recommended
You're pregnant or breastfeeding. You have type 1 diabetes or take insulin. You have a history of eating disorders. In these cases — skip this module entirely. The other 11 modules are enough.

How to start
without suffering.
The only way that works long-term: gradually.
Week 1 — 12/12: just skip the after-dinner snack
Finish dinner. Close the kitchen. Nothing until breakfast. Most people are already close to 12/12 — this makes it conscious and consistent.
Week 2 — 14/10: push breakfast back by 2 hours
If you normally eat at 7am, start at 9am. Black coffee or tea is allowed — zero calories. Water is always allowed. Most hunger in the morning is habit, not biology.
Week 3+ — 16/8: the full window
If breakfast was at 9am, first meal is now 11am–12pm. Eat normally within the 8-hour window. No calorie restriction. No food rules. Just timing.
💧 During the fasting window: water, black coffee, plain tea, sparkling water — all fine. The goal is keeping insulin low. Any calories — even "just milk in coffee" — break the metabolic benefit.
