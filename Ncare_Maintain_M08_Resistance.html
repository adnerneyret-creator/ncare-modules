Why this matters for maintenance
Muscle burns calories at rest. Every kg of muscle you carry burns ~150 extra calories per day — without any effort. It's a metabolic engine running 24/7.
GLP-1 causes muscle loss. Without resistance training during treatment, 25–40% of weight lost is muscle — not fat. This lowers your resting metabolism and makes rebound more likely.
2 sessions per week is enough. Minimum effective dose for muscle maintenance: 2×20 minutes of resistance work. No gym needed. Body weight is sufficient.

Form over speed.
Every time.
Three form rules that prevent 90% of home workout injuries.
Squats — knees track over toes
Push your knees outward — never let them cave inward. Go only as deep as comfortable. Depth comes with practice. A shallow squat done safely beats a deep squat done wrong.
Push-ups — one straight line
Head, shoulders, hips, ankles — one line. If hips sag, drop to knees. The modification is not a failure. It's the correct regression. Build the line, then build the reps.
Plank — breathe, don't hold
Most people hold their breath in a plank. This spikes blood pressure unnecessarily. Breathe normally throughout. If you can't breathe, shorten the hold — don't stop breathing.
💡 Progressive overload: Add 1 rep per set each week. Not more. Consistency over months beats intensity for one session. Muscle is built in the adaptation phase — not during the workout.
How to progress
without getting hurt.
One rule. Simple enough to never overthink.
The 2-sessions-per-week rule: never add intensity and frequency at the same time. Increase one, keep the other constant. Your muscles need 48 hours to recover between sessions.
Month-by-month progression
Month 1: 2×/week · 3 sets · 8–10 reps · Body weight only. Learn the movements. Don't rush.
Month 2: 2×/week · 3 sets · 12–15 reps · Add a 5th exercise (lunges). Increase range of motion.
Month 3: 2–3×/week · 4 sets · Add resistance (backpack with books, resistance bands). Muscle memory is established.
Signs you're overdoing it
Soreness lasting more than 3 days after a session → reduce volume by 30%.
Joint pain (not muscle fatigue) → stop that exercise and simplify the movement.
Dreading sessions every time → reduce frequency to 1×/week until enjoyment returns.
