Why after — not before
Muscles act as glucose sponges. When you walk right after eating, your muscles absorb glucose directly from the bloodstream — before insulin needs to store it as fat.
The window is 30 minutes. The glucose spike peaks around 30–45 minutes after a meal. Walking in this window intercepts it at its source.
No effort required. A gentle pace works. You don't need to sweat. The effect is mechanical — muscles pulling glucose — not cardiovascular.
Source: Buffey A.J. et al., Sports Medicine (2022) · DiPietro L. et al., Diabetes Care (2013)
The only 3 rules.
Simple enough to never have an excuse.
1. Start within 30 minutes of finishing your meal
You don't need to time it perfectly. Just don't sit down for 30 minutes after eating. The window is generous.
2. Walk for exactly 15 minutes
Not 5. Not 45. 15 is the minimum effective dose. More is fine — but 15 is enough. This boundary removes the "I don't have time" objection permanently.
3. No running shoes required
Socks at home, shoes outside. Around the block, in a corridor, on a balcony. The terrain doesn't matter. The timing does.
💡 Can't walk? Light housework, standing, gentle stretching — any movement activates the same glucose absorption mechanism. The goal is muscle contraction, not steps.
