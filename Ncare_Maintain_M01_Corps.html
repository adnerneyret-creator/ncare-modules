What's actually happening
Hunger hormones bounce back. Ghrelin — the hunger hormone — rises above pre-treatment levels when GLP-1 stops. Your brain receives stronger hunger signals than before.
Metabolism adapts. Your body learned to be efficient at a lower weight. It now burns fewer calories to maintain that weight — and resists change.
This is not failure. It's a predictable biological response. And predictable means manageable.
What GLP-1 did for you.
Understanding the tool — so you can work without it.
GLP-1 acted on 3 systems simultaneously
Appetite suppression. Slowed gastric emptying — food stayed in your stomach longer. Fullness came earlier and lasted longer.
Blood sugar regulation. Reduced post-meal glucose spikes — less insulin released, less fat stored.
Reward pathway reset. Reduced the dopamine response to food — cravings felt less intense. The brain's "food as reward" signal was quieter.
Without GLP-1, these systems return to their baseline. Maintain's job is to rebuild them through habits — not medication.

Four levers. One goal: keep what you built.
🌙 Sleep — your metabolic reset button
Poor sleep raises ghrelin and lowers leptin — exactly the combination that drives rebound. 7–9h of quality sleep is non-negotiable for weight maintenance.
🚶 Movement — your insulin sensitivity shield
15 minutes of walking after meals blunts glucose spikes by up to 30%. It's the single most effective non-medication tool for metabolic maintenance.
🍽️ Nutrition — eating to stay, not to lose
Protein and fibre trigger satiety hormones. Building meals around these two nutrients recreates — naturally — what GLP-1 did pharmacologically.
🧠 Stress management — your cortisol firewall
Cortisol drives fat storage — especially abdominal. Chronic stress undoes metabolic progress faster than any dietary slip. Managing stress is managing weight.
