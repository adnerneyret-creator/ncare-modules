The vagus nerve.
Your built-in off switch.
It connects your brain to your gut, heart, and lungs. And you can activate it voluntarily.
Two modes. One switch.
Sympathetic (stress mode): heart rate up, digestion off, muscles tense, cortisol rising. Triggered by short inhales, shallow breathing, held breath.
Parasympathetic (recovery mode): heart rate down, digestion on, muscles relax, cortisol drops. Triggered by slow, extended exhales.
Why the exhale is the key
When you exhale slowly, your heart rate decreases. This activates the vagus nerve. Which signals the brain: the threat is over. Cortisol drops within 90 seconds.
Cardiac coherence — breathing at exactly 5 breaths per minute — synchronises heart and brain rhythms. Effect lasts 4–6 hours after a single 5-minute session.
Source: Lehrer P.M. et al., Applied Psychophysiology & Biofeedback (2014) · Porges S.W., Polyvagal Theory (2011)
Three protocols.
One for every situation.
Different needs. Different rhythms. All scientifically validated.
Cardiac coherence — 5-5 · Daily maintenance
Inhale 5 seconds, exhale 5 seconds. 5 minutes = 5 breaths/min. The gold standard for HRV improvement and cortisol regulation. Use morning, midday, evening.
4-7-8 — Acute stress response
Inhale 4 sec, hold 7 sec, exhale 8 sec. Activates the parasympathetic system rapidly. Use before a difficult conversation, presentation, or moment of panic.
Physiological sigh — Emergency reset
Double inhale through the nose (sniff-sniff), then one long exhale through the mouth. Instantly deflates the lungs' air sacs, rapidly activating the vagus nerve. Works in 1 breath.
💡 The 3-3-3 rule: 3 sessions per day, 3 minutes minimum, 3 weeks to feel the structural change. Before that — you'll feel calmer after each session. The baseline shifts over weeks.
