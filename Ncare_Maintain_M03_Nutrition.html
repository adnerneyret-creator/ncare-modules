Two nutrients do 80% of the work. Protein and fibre. Everything else is detail.

Why this ratio works for maintenance
Protein — triggers satiety hormones (PYY, GLP-1 naturally) and preserves muscle mass. Aim for 1.2–1.6g per kg of body weight daily.
Fibre — slows glucose absorption, feeds gut bacteria that regulate appetite hormones. 25–35g per day is the maintenance target.
Complex carbs — provide sustained energy without the insulin spike that drives fat storage. Oats, sweet potato, legumes.
3 myths that cause rebound.
You've probably heard all of them.
MYTH
"Eating less is always better for maintaining weight."
FACT
Eating too little triggers metabolic adaptation — your body lowers its calorie burn. This is the #1 cause of rebound. Eating enough keeps your metabolism active.
MYTH
"Carbs are the enemy of weight maintenance."
FACT
Refined carbs spike insulin. Complex carbs fuel muscle and brain without the spike. The type matters — not the category. Removing all carbs causes cravings that lead to bingeing.
MYTH
"If I slip once, I've failed and should restart."
FACT
One meal changes nothing. One week of meals changes something. The "all or nothing" mindset is the most documented driver of weight cycling. Progress is a trend, not a straight line.
Quick check.
Which meal better supports weight maintenance?
🥗 Small salad with light dressing, no protein
Low protein = hunger returns fast. Light = metabolism gets no signal to stay active. This is a diet meal — not a maintenance meal.
🍗 Chicken + roasted vegetables + lentils
Protein (chicken) + fibre (lentils) + complex carbs (vegetables). This meal activates satiety hormones, stabilises blood sugar, and keeps metabolism running. ✓
🍞 Whole wheat toast with low-fat cheese
Better than refined carbs, but insufficient protein and fibre. Blood sugar will drop within 2 hours, triggering hunger and cravings.
MAINTAIN · The 80/20 rule
Perfect doesn't
exist. Consistent does.
You don't need to eat perfectly. You need to eat well 80% of the time. The other 20% is called life — and it's allowed.
Your one action for tonight
Look at what you're planning for dinner. Can you add one source of protein and one source of fibre? That's it. Not a new diet. Just two ingredients.
