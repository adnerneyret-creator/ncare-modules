Exercise spikes cortisol.
Then drops it below baseline.
That's the mechanism. And it only works if you let it complete.
The cortisol curve of exercise
During exercise: cortisol rises — this is normal and necessary. It mobilises energy stores and prepares the body for effort.
30–60 min after: cortisol drops significantly below your pre-exercise level. This is the recovery dividend — and why regular exercisers have lower baseline stress.
The problem: if you exercise at maximum intensity when already stressed, cortisol spikes so high it doesn't fully recover. You feel worse, not better.
When you're burned out: moderate intensity is more effective than high intensity. Walking, yoga, swimming, cycling at a conversational pace. The goal is the recovery drop — not performance.
The right exercise
for your stress level.
Not all movement is equal when cortisol is already high.
PSS-10 below 14 — You have capacity
Any exercise works. HIIT, resistance training, running. Your cortisol is low enough that the spike from intense exercise will fully recover. Add intensity progressively.
PSS-10 14–26 — Moderate stress
Moderate intensity only. 20–30 min walks, yoga, swimming, light cycling. Resistance training 2×/week — not more. This is the sweet spot for cortisol reduction without overloading the system.
PSS-10 above 26 — High stress
Gentle only. Walking, stretching, slow yoga. No HIIT. No intense cardio. Your cortisol is already near its ceiling — adding exercise cortisol on top makes it worse. Recovery first, performance later.
💡 Your PSS-10 score from your weekly check-in tells your AI which zone you're in. Recommendations adapt automatically — you don't need to track this yourself.

SÉRÉNITÉ · Movement as medicine
The goal isn't fitness.
It's a lower
cortisol baseline.
Every session of this circuit is a cortisol intervention. Not a workout. Not a punishment. A physiological reset that takes 20 minutes and works better than most supplements.
Your movement prescription
Frequency: 3×/week minimum. Not every day — recovery days matter as much as active days.
Timing: morning or early afternoon. Evening intense exercise delays sleep and raises evening cortisol.
Signal to watch: if you feel worse after exercise (not just tired) — reduce intensity. That's your body telling you cortisol is already too high.
