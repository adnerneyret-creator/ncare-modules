95% of serotonin
is made in your gut.
The gut-brain axis runs both ways. What you eat changes how you feel — within hours.
The direct pathway
Gut microbiome → neurotransmitters. Your gut bacteria produce serotonin, GABA, and dopamine precursors. Feed the right bacteria and mood chemistry improves measurably.
Blood sugar → cortisol. Every glucose spike triggers an insulin response. When blood sugar crashes, cortisol rises to compensate. Stable blood sugar = lower baseline anxiety.
Inflammation → depression. Inflammatory foods (sugar, seed oils, ultra-processed) activate cytokines that cross the blood-brain barrier and directly impair mood regulation.
You don't need a special diet. You need to shift the ratio — more foods that stabilise blood sugar and feed beneficial gut bacteria. Fewer that spike insulin and inflame.

Sedates initially, then fragments sleep and suppresses REM. Cortisol rebounds at 3am — the classic "anxiety at 3am" pattern.
You feel anxious at 3pm every day. What's most likely causing it?
One right answer. Think about the timing.
Too much screen time in the afternoon
Screens contribute to stress but don't explain the precise 3pm timing. The pattern is too regular to be random.
A blood sugar crash from lunch triggering a cortisol spike
Correct. A high-carb lunch (without enough protein) causes a glucose spike around noon, followed by an insulin-driven crash 2–3 hours later. Cortisol rises to bring blood sugar back up — and you feel it as anxiety. Fix: add protein and fat to lunch. ✓
Work stress accumulating through the day
Work stress is real but produces a gradual rise, not a precise 3pm spike. The regularity of the pattern points to a physiological — not psychological — trigger.

This meal stabilises blood sugar for 4+ hours, delivers omega-3 and magnesium, and feeds beneficial gut bacteria. Make it once a week — your 3pm will change.
🥗 The Sérénité bowl
⏱ 10 min · 1 serving · Cortisol-stabilising
100g tinned salmon or sardines
Large handful spinach or rocket
½ avocado (magnesium + healthy fat)
Handful blueberries or raspberries
10 almonds, olive oil, lemon
Combine. Dress with olive oil and lemon. Done. Protein + omega-3 + magnesium + anthocyanins + fibre — everything your nervous system needs in one bowl.
