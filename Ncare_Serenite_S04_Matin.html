Your morning is the

What you do in the first 10 minutes changes everything that follows.
What most people do
Check phone immediately. News, emails, social media. The brain receives stress signals before it's even fully awake. Cortisol spikes unnecessarily.
Rush mode activated. No buffer, no transition. The nervous system enters threat mode from the first seconds of the day — and never fully exits.
What 10 intentional minutes do
Cortisol Awakening Response (CAR): cortisol naturally peaks 20–30 min after waking. What you do in this window determines how that peak lands — productively or anxiously.
Natural light + movement + breathing in the first 10 minutes sets your circadian rhythm, blunts the anxiety edge of the cortisol peak, and lowers baseline stress all day.
Source: Wust S. et al., Psychoneuroendocrinology (2000) · Huberman A. et al., Stanford Neuroscience (2021)
Build your anchor.
Pick 3 habits. That's your 10-minute morning. No more, no less.
2 min
No phone for 10 minutes after waking
The single highest-leverage change. Your brain needs a stress-free window to complete its sleep cycle properly.
✓
3 min
Natural light exposure
Open a window or step outside. 3 minutes of natural light synchronises your circadian clock and improves sleep quality that same night.
✓
3 min
Cardiac coherence breathing
5-5 breathing before the day starts. Blunts the anxiety edge of the morning cortisol peak.
✓
2 min
Drink 500ml water immediately
You wake up mildly dehydrated. Cortisol rises with dehydration. Water first, coffee second — even 2 minutes delay makes a measurable difference.
✓
5 min
Gentle movement — stretching or walking
Activates the lymphatic system and uses up excess cortisol constructively. Doesn't need to be intense — intentional is enough.
✓
2 min
Name one intention for the day
Not a to-do list. One sentence: what matters most today. Prefrontal cortex activation before the amygdala takes over.

SÉRÉNITÉ · Daily anchor
10 minutes.
Every morning.
Before anything else.
The morning anchor doesn't require motivation. It requires a decision made the night before — phone face down, glass of water on the nightstand, alarm 10 minutes earlier.
The one rule that makes it work
The anchor must happen before your first screen exposure. Once you check your phone, the cortisol window is already determined. The anchor only works in the first 10 minutes — before the day's inputs arrive.
