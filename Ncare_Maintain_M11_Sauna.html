4 repair mechanisms
Heat shock proteins (HSPs) — repair damaged proteins, protect muscle fibres from breakdown, improve insulin receptor function.
Growth hormone surge — preserves muscle mass and mobilises fat stores during the session. The spike lasts 2–3 hours post-sauna.
Cortisol reduction — core body temperature rise followed by cool-down mimics the natural cortisol rhythm, helping to lower evening cortisol and improve sleep onset.
BDNF increase — brain-derived neurotrophic factor rises after heat exposure — improving mood, reducing stress eating, and supporting cognitive function.
Source: Laukkanen J.A. et al., JAMA Internal Medicine (2015) · Scoon G.S. et al., J Sci Med Sport (2007) · Patrick R.P. et al., Med Hypotheses (2021)
The protocol.
Simple and effective.
20 minutes. 2–3 times per week. That's the minimum effective dose.
The session structure
Enter at 70–90°C. Sit or lie comfortably. Stay for 15–20 minutes. Exit and cool down for 5–10 minutes (cold shower optional but powerful — see Module 07). Rehydrate with 500ml water.
Timing for metabolism
Post-exercise sauna amplifies growth hormone release by up to 16× compared to sauna alone. If you've done your Module 08 circuit, a sauna within 30 minutes maximises the anabolic window.
Timing for sleep
Evening sauna (6–8pm) causes the core temperature rise and fall that signals deep sleep onset. Many people report falling asleep faster and sleeping deeper on sauna days.
⚠️ Safety: No sauna if pregnant, if you have uncontrolled hypertension, or recent cardiovascular event. Always exit if you feel dizzy or nauseous. Hydrate before and after.

No sauna?
No problem.
Four alternatives that activate the same heat pathways — accessible anywhere.
🛁
Hot bath — 40°C for 20 minutes
Core temperature rise similar to sauna. Add Epsom salts for magnesium absorption — relaxes muscles and supports sleep. Studies show equivalent cardiovascular benefits to moderate exercise.
Best alternative
🧖
Hammam / steam room
Lower temperature (45–50°C) but high humidity increases perceived heat load. Easier to tolerate for beginners. Available in most public pools and sports centres.
Good option
🚿
Contrast shower — hot/cold cycling
2 min hot, 30 sec cold × 3 rounds. Activates both heat shock proteins AND cold thermogenesis (Module 07). The most accessible protocol if you have no other option.
Daily option
🧘
Infrared sauna blanket
Home infrared devices reach 55–65°C and penetrate tissue differently. Increasingly accessible (£100–200). Studies show similar cortisol reduction and growth hormone benefits.
